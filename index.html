ABOUT

Kari, formerly known as Crunchyverse, first started seriously exploring music around 2022–2023. Growing up, he wasn’t heavily exposed to music—he only had a Samsung Galaxy S5 and limited internet access. Early inspirations included Marshmello and Alan Walker from a mobile game, but these had little impact initially.

In late 2023, Kari began collaborating with friends Pluto and Haylocon in their own version of “Song Wars.” His first track, s3lf-destruction, was a rough breakcore experiment. Soon after, he recorded Hands Up! and Heartsunk, exploring experimental production techniques.

During the “Paranoid Era,” Kari embraced post-punk, alt-rock, and shoegaze influences, releasing tracks like Haunt Me and the Vampire demo. Visual experimentation included black-and-white clothing and gothic accessories, which became a humorous inside joke among peers.

In 2024, Kari met Jordan (stage name Kami, formerly Sensei Jay), and together with Pluto, they formed the collective TMR (The Midnight Riders). Collaborative releases like Stuck on You, I’ll Make You Mine, and 2:30 AM showcased his strengths as both a singer and rapper.

By March 2025, Kari released his first full-length album, Karikore, blending house, UK garage, new jazz, and more. Tracks like Dy Wna Fall 4 Me and Y U Playin / Hoop Dreams highlight his versatility and embrace of underground influences like Jaydes.

Currently, Kari is preparing his second album, Bloom, aiming to expand his sound while maintaining his distinctive Y2K-inspired aesthetic. With TMR and collaborators, he continues to push boundaries, blending genres and creating innovative music.
